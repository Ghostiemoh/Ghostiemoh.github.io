How Do They Work? Launch Mechanisms Explained

Model 1: The Curated IDO (Initial DEX Offering)

Used by platforms like Solanium, this model focuses on vetting projects and offering tokens at a fixed price to whitelisted investors, often requiring users to stake the launchpad's native token.

1. Project Vetting

Rigorous review of team, tech, and tokenomics.

→
2. Staking & Whitelist

Investors stake tokens (e.g., SLIM) for a lottery or guaranteed allocation.

→
3. Fixed Price Sale

Whitelisted users buy tokens at a set price.

→
4. Token Distribution

Tokens are airdropped to participants' wallets, often with vesting.

Model 2: The Fair Launch Bonding Curve

Pioneered on Solana by Pump.fun, this model automates price discovery and liquidity creation. There are no presales; everyone buys from the same curve.

1. Instant Creation

Anyone creates a token with a name, ticker, and image for a small SOL fee.

→
2. Bonding Curve Trading

Price increases as more people buy. All SOL goes into the curve.

→
3. Market Cap Goal

When the market cap hits a target (e.g., ~$69k), a portion of SOL is used for DEX liquidity.

→
4. DEX Graduation

Liquidity is created on a DEX (e.g., Raydium) and LP tokens are burned, making it permanent.

Model 3: Futarchy & Market-Based Governance

The experimental model from MetaDAO. It's not a typical launchpad; it's a way to launch a DAO where decisions are made by betting on outcomes in prediction markets, not by voting.

1. Proposal is Made

A proposal is submitted to the DAO (e.g., "fund development of feature X").

→
2. Prediction Markets Open

Two markets are created: one for if the proposal PASSES, one for if it FAILS.

→
3. Market Trading

Participants trade on the future value of the DAO's token based on which outcome they think is better.

→
4. Decision is Automated

The outcome (PASS/FAIL) that the market predicts will result in a higher token price is automatically executed.
